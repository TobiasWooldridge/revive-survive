Revive Survive

Don't risk fatigue on our roads. Know where and when to stop.

Revive Survive runs constantly on your device, and knows when you're driving. It tells you:

  • Where and when to stop
  • When you are approaching an area of higher crash risk
  • Where upcoming public toilets, rest stops and hotels are

Run it all the time - it won't drain your battery.

After 3 hours, drivers are reminded of the road toll and fatigue statistics.

Tech

Instead of using GPS or WiFi to know what you're doing, Revive Survive uses Android's Activity Recognition service. This uses the phone's accelerometer to know whether you're driving or not, and uses less power than the GPS.

The application currently gets upcoming rest stops from a PostGis server. An enhancement could store this data locally so no data connection is needed.

The GPS is activated briefly when it's time to stop driving. The low-resolution service which uses the mobile phone towers would be adequate.

Data sources

Our data sources include:

  • Road crashes in SA
  • Roadside significant sites
  • Rest areas
  • National public toilet map
  • Picnic tables (QLD)
  • Public barbecues (QLD)
  • Public barbecues (Ballarat)
  • Public barbecues (Geelong)
  • Petrol stations, public toilets and hotels from OpenStreetMap
  • Petrol stations GPS data provided by BP
  • Stop. Revive. Survive. Rest stop map (fatigue statistic)
  • South Australian Road Crash Statistics (serious injuries and fatalities)

These datasets could also be useful:

  • Australian Tourism Data Warehouse API
  • City of Melbourne street furniture
